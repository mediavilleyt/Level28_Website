Github Copilot is an open source software that helps you to create code. It automatically generates what it thinks you want to type next. You'll need a student account or a subscription to use it in your code editor.

ChatGPT

ChatGPT is an open source software, a language model made by OpenAI. Its purpose is to assist users with text-based prompts, generating suitable responses. It's free and can be used by people of any age; you just need to create a free account.

DALL-E is an open source software that specializes in image generation, also made by OpenAI. There are different versions, including DALL-E 2 and DALL-E 3. DALL-E 2 is currently free, while DALL-E requires payment.

Training

ChatGPT's training process involves several key steps to equip it with the ability to understand and generate natural language effectively:

  • Data Collection: The first step in training ChatGPT is to gather a diverse and extensive dataset comprising a wide range of text sources. These sources may include books, articles, websites, forums, social media posts, and more. The goal is to expose ChatGPT to as much linguistic diversity and real-world context as possible.
  • Preprocessing: Once the data is collected, it undergoes preprocessing to clean and format it for training. This may involve tasks such as removing irrelevant content, standardizing formatting, tokenizing text into words or subwords, and segmenting sentences or paragraphs.
  • Tokenization: In tokenization, each word or subword in the text is assigned a unique numerical identifier, known as a token. This process converts raw text into a format that can be processed by machine learning algorithms. Tokenization is essential for representing the text in a way that the model can understand and learn from.
  • Architecture Selection: ChatGPT is based on the Transformer architecture, specifically the GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) variant. The Transformer architecture is well-suited for modeling sequential data like text and has shown remarkable performance in natural language processing tasks.
  • Model Training: With the data prepared and the architecture selected, the training process begins. During training, the model learns to predict the next word in a sequence given the preceding words. This is achieved through a process called self-supervised learning, where the model is trained to minimize the difference between its predictions and the actual next words in the training data.
  • Objective Function: The training objective for ChatGPT is typically based on maximizing the likelihood of generating the correct next word in a sequence. This is often formalized using techniques such as maximum likelihood estimation or other probabilistic frameworks.
  • Fine-tuning: After the initial pre-training phase, ChatGPT can be further fine-tuned on specific tasks or domains to improve its performance. Fine-tuning involves exposing the model to additional data relevant to the target task and adjusting its parameters to better align with the task's objectives.
  • Evaluation: Throughout the training process, the performance of ChatGPT is evaluated on held-out validation data to monitor its progress and prevent overfitting. Various metrics, such as perplexity or accuracy, may be used to assess the model's performance.

By undergoing these steps, ChatGPT is trained to understand and generate natural language in a way that captures the nuances and complexities of human communication. Its training process is iterative and computationally intensive, requiring significant computational resources and expertise in machine learning techniques. However, the resulting model is capable of producing human-like text and performing a wide range of natural language processing tasks with impressive accuracy and fluency.

Generating

Generating When a question is asked or an instruction is provided, ChatGPT analyzes the input to understand the context. It then generates a suitable response using its knowledge and understanding of language. Responses are generated based on probability, taking into account the context and the input it has received.

How Code is Used

In responses, ChatGPT often uses code snippets to clarify concepts, provide examples, or suggest solutions to specific problems. This can range from simple script examples to more complex algorithms, depending on the question and the topic of the conversation.
